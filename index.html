Heatwave · first 48 hours

34–36°C Mon 13 and Tue 14, dropping to ~28°C from Wednesday. Almost nothing in Paris has A/C. Museums and shade in the afternoon, walking after 7pm. Refill water free at the green Wallace fountains — they're everywhere.

You'll miss the fireworks — and that's not your fault

Paris moved the Eiffel Tower display to Monday the 13th this year, so the 14th can mark ten years since the Nice attack. It goes off at 11pm Paris time — you'll be mid-Atlantic. Every guide still says "July 14". They're wrong this year. The tower still sparkles for five minutes on the hour, every night, all summer.

Arrival day is a Tuesday, and Tuesday is a trap

You land ~9am on the 14th. The Louvre, Orangerie and Bourse de Commerce are all closed on Tuesdays — and it's a national holiday on top. Orsay and Rodin are open, but you'll be jetlagged in 34°C heat. Day one is for arriving, not sightseeing.

Heatstroke is not tiredness

If you stop sweating, feel dizzy, get a pounding headache or feel confused — that's heatstroke. Get indoors, cold water on your skin and neck, call 15. Don't wait to see if it passes.

Getting home at night

  • Last métro ≈ 12:45am Sun–Thu, ≈ 2am Fri & Sat. It's posted on the platform — read it when you arrive, not when you leave.
  • Noctilien night buses run all night after the métro stops.
  • Screenshot your route home before you go out. A screenshot needs no signal.
  • Set a leave alarm. You will not want to do the maths at 1am.
  • Send me the name of the bar before you walk in. Costs you nothing.
